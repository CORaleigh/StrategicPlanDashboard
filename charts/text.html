Affordable Housing Strategies

Decent, affordable housing is a critical part of any community’s health. It is important to the economic vitality as affordable homes can attract and retain employees to our community, which can be a selling point and a competitive advantage for area employers. Affordable homes also allow the local workforce to live near their jobs. Shorter commutes assist with reducing traffic congestion, air pollution, and expenditures on roads. The benefits of affordable housing are numerous.

Like many other large U.S. cities, Raleigh’s housing costs continue to rise. Raleigh City Council took a proactive approach to addressing the community’s affordable housing needs by approving the Affordable Housing Improvement Plan in fall 2015 and in 2016, adding one cent to the property tax rate, equaling $5.7 million, to implement the Plan. The new revenues will primarily support Low Income Housing Tax Credit projects.

The contract for 11 city-owned parcels to be converted into affordable housing is currently being routed. Staff is currently in the process of accepting proposals for mixed-income East College Park home construction. More than 140 residences, including single family and multi-family townhomes, will be built.

Racially Concentrated Areas of Poverty (RCAPs)

There are nine Racially Concentrated Areas of Poverty (RCAPs) in Raleigh. The nine RCAPs are highlighted in the map to the right. RCAPs are defined as a Census Tract that exhibits both of these characteristics according to Census data: (1) more than 30% of residents live below poverty level and (2) more than 50% of households are non-white.
